Historical and Cultural Background of Kandy

In the period of World War II, Sri Lanka was a base for the allied forces and in 1944 the South East Asian Command of allies were known to have moved the base to Kandy until the end of the war. In October 1947, the country gained Independence from the British and their on forth developed under local democratic rule. The main structures, from homes to shops that still exist in the central business district of Kandy belong to the times of British rule. Over the years the city has modified and modernized and evolved into what it is today. Due to the presence of the Sri Dalada Maligawa as well as the cities other historical and cultural sites it can truly be deemed a unique and important city.
