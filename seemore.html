Udawatta Kele Sanctuary, frequently spelled as Udawattakele, is a very popular and historic forest reserve on the hilly side of the city of Kandy. During the days where kings ruled Kandy, Udawatta Kele was known as "Uda Wasala Watta", in Sinhala it meant "the garden situated above the royal palace". The sanctuary is famous for its wide-ranging species of birds found here. The reserve also contains a great variety of plant species. Many of small and medium size mammals that live in Sri Lanka can be seen here. Several kinds of snakes and other reptiles also could be seen. Udawatta Kele was nominated as a forest reserve in 1856, and later it became a sanctuary in 1938. Since it could be easily visited and it contains great variety of fauna and flora the forest has a great educational value. The forest is very popular with foreign tourists, especially people enjoying bird watching. The forest is also has a significant religious importance since there are three Buddhist meditation hermitages in the reserve.
